What Should I Do After an Accident? Immediately after an accident, the first thing that you should do is to check if you, your passengers and any other party in the accident has sustained serious injuries. If there are any people injured at the scene of the accident, call medical personnel immediately. You should then call the police to report the accident. Do not leave the scene of the accident until the police have arrived and recorded your statement on what transpired.
If you have not been seriously injured, you should get out of your vehicle and take photographs or a video of the scene of the accident. The pictures that you take will act as crucial evidence when your lawyer is filing a crash claim. You should also take the contact information of all drivers involved in the accident as well the contact information of all witnesses who saw how the accident transpired. You can also insurance information with the other drivers involved in the accident. These details will help your lawyer create a more formidable case against the at-fault party.
Immediately after an accident, do not discuss how you are feeling with other drivers who are involved in the crash. In fact, you should only talk to your lawyer. If you share any details on how you are feeling with the at-fault driver, they can end up using this information against you later in court. After the police have taken down your report, you should ensure that you visit a hospital even if you think you are not seriously injured. Remember that some injuries take time to manifest and you may have sustained serious internal injuries in the crash.
